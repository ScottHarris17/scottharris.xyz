What's Scott's background?
Scott is a computational neuroscientist with expertise in vision science and neural computation. He's published research in eLife on retinal image stabilization, studied glaucoma's effects on neural circuits, and received the Copenhagen Award in 2024. Beyond research, he builds scientific software tools and writes about consciousness and free will.

“Understanding vision requires building the tools to see it clearly.”

Scott C. Harris is a computational neuroscientist whose work sits at the intersection of biology, engineering, and philosophy. His research focuses on how the retina processes visual information — from the mechanics of image stabilization to the neural consequences of glaucoma. He develops open-source scientific software to make vision research more accessible and reproducible, and writes essays exploring consciousness, free will, and what it means to understand the mind.
