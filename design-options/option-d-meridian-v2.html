AI Scott — Ask me anything
What does Scott do?
Scott is a computational neuroscientist turned AI leader. He's currently CTO of a psychology startup where he built the full stack for automating practices with AI. Previously at Eon Systems, he led the creation of the first multi-behavior brain upload using the drosophila connectome and spiking neural networks — that project got 30 million impressions in its first week. He also developed a diagnostic tool for neurodegenerative diseases with UCSF.
Show me his projects
Here are some highlights: Bassoon — psychophysics platform, Electrophysiology Pipeline — neural data analysis, Calibrate Light — photoisomerization calculator. He's also built interactive demos like a Viral Spread Simulator and Interest Calculator. Want to know more about any of these?

“From neural circuits to neural networks — understanding intelligence requires building it.”

Scott C. Harris is a computational neuroscientist and technologist whose career spans from retinal electrophysiology to leading AI product development. As CTO of a psychology startup, he built the full stack for automating clinical practices with AI. At Eon Systems, he led the first multi-behavior brain upload using the complete drosophila connectome. His research at UCSF produced a new diagnostic tool for neurodegenerative diseases, alongside published work on retinal computation and the Copenhagen Award in 2024.
