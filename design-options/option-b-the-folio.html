“The intersection of neuroscience and computation is where understanding begins.”

Scott C. Harris holds a PhD in computational neuroscience and has dedicated his career to understanding how the visual system processes and stabilizes the world we see. His research, published in eLife and recognized with the 2024 Copenhagen Award, bridges experimental neuroscience and quantitative modeling.

Beyond the lab, Scott builds research software that other scientists rely on daily. Bassoon enables precise psychophysics experiments, his Electrophysiology Pipeline streamlines neural data analysis, and Calibrate Light simplifies complex photometric calculations.

He also writes long-form essays exploring consciousness, free will, and the philosophical implications of brain science. His work lives at the boundary of what we can measure and what we struggle to explain.
